How it works

Variables

  • \(n\) — number of circles (iterations), \(n \ge 1\)
  • \(d\) — size divisor, \(d > 1\)
  • \(m\) — angle multiplier, \(m \ne 0\)
  • \(i\) — current iteration (0-indexed)
  • \(r\) — radius of the outermost circle
  • \((x_0,\,y_0)\) — centre of the outermost circle

Circle chain

Each circle’s centre sits on the rim of the previous one. As \(\theta\) sweeps \([0,2\pi]\) the innermost centre traces the pattern (red dot above, with n=3, d=2, m=2).

Step by step

Centre of circle 2  (\(i=0\)):

\[\begin{aligned} x_2 &= x_0 - \!\left(r \pm \frac{r}{d}\right)\!\cos\theta \\ y_2 &= y_0 - \!\left(r \pm \frac{r}{d}\right)\!\sin\theta \end{aligned}\]

Centre of circle 3  (\(i=1\)):

\[\begin{aligned} x_3 &= x_2 - \!\left(\frac{r}{d} \pm \frac{r}{d^2}\right)\!\cos(m\theta) \\ y_3 &= y_2 - \!\left(\frac{r}{d} \pm \frac{r}{d^2}\right)\!\sin(m\theta) \end{aligned}\]

There is a clear pattern above.

General form

The path traced by the innermost circle’s centre as \(\theta\) sweeps \([0,\,2\pi]\):

\[\begin{aligned} x &= x_0 - \sum_{i\,=\,0}^{n-2}\!\left(\frac{r}{d^{\,i}} \pm \frac{r}{d^{\,i+1}}\right)\cos\!\left(m^i\theta\right) \\ y &= y_0 - \sum_{i\,=\,0}^{n-2}\!\left(\frac{r}{d^{\,i}} \pm \frac{r}{d^{\,i+1}}\right)\sin\!\left(m^i\theta\right) \end{aligned}\]

where \(0 \le \theta \le 2\pi\), and \(\pm\) depends on whether the circles are placed outside (\(+\)) or nested inside (\(-\)) one another.
